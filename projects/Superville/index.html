Landscapes

The surrounding side valleys of Tyrol are increasingly struggling with an exodus of the population. Business and industry are shifting almost entirely to the Inn Valley. This has resulted in a reduction in the cultural care of the Lech Valley, because only where land is used, it is also adequately cared for. However, the topographical conditions in the Lech Valley require particularly intensive landscape maintenance. This is because there is a particularly high density of avalanches and floods. This project shows how the Lech Valley could continue to be preserved as an attractive and productive landscape. Natural occurrence of medicinal plants is rapidly decreasing. In addition, synthesis of some natural drugs is not possible until today. Through cultivation roboter and intelligent temporary drying shells, natural plants could be cultivated and serve a new purpose in the Lechtal valley. The Lechtal Limestone Alps have particularly suitable conditions for their cultivation. Essential factors are soil quality, water conditions, exposure of the steep slopes and their machinability, logical divisions of the fields and their planting result. The drying shells retain their temporary character. As such they preserve living space for future population in a productive way.
